Financial Aid at The Herald

“The Herald's financial assistance program allowed me to embark on one of the greatest learning experiences of my life.”

— Chris Hatfield '06, Executive Editor

"The Herald's financial assistance program gave me the breathing room to become more involved with campus journalism. Working at The Herald was one of the most rewarding and educational opportunities I have ever had. Without the help of the paper's alums and the financial assistance program, I would not have been able to have the wonderful experiences that I did.”

— Ross Frazier '09, Editor-in-Chief

The Herald is produced entirely by a volunteer student staff. Given the significant time committment required at The Herald, some students need to make difficult decisions about how much time they can devote to The Herald.

Funded initially by a general Herald alum, the financial aid program begin in 2006. Stipends of between $400 and $1,250 are awarded to students on a need-based, confidential basis.

Gifts of any amount support this program, and all gifts to The Herald are tax deductible. A gift of $17,500 would endow one semester of stipends, and $500,000 would permanently endow the program.
