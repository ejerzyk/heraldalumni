Digitizing The Herald's Archives

Daily since 1891, The Herald remains the paper of record for the Brown University community. For more than 125 years, The Herald has published daily campus news, capturing life at Brown University and student perspectives on world events. Now, The Herald seeks to make this incomparable history of Brown – written and produced by students since the paper’s founding – publicly available online. Past issues of The Herald are a valuable, but frequently untapped, resource for journalists, historians and others interested in exploring Brown University’s history.

When digitization is complete, former Herald writers will be able to access every article they’ve ever written with a click; current students will be able to search for any subject in The Herald over the span of more than a century; and historians, scholars and novelists will be able to read about the thoughts and lives of Brunonians over the course of two World Wars, the Civil Rights movement, man’s first walk on the Moon and September 11, 2001.
